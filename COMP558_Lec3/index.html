Image smoothing

  • to reduce noise, one often smooths the image by averaging intensities (RGBs)

  • when we do averaging in MatLab, since they assume out-of-boundary values are 0

We can also smooth over a larger neighbourhood!!!

How to choose weights of smoothing function?

Gaussian function

  • integrates to 1, because of normalization constant
  • often called normal distribution
  • can use it for smoothing weights

Properties of Gaussian

  • 95% within ±2±2±2﻿ 𝛔
  • 98% within ±3±3±3﻿ 𝛔

bigger sigma, lower probability to get out of bound

Local difference

Often one would like to enhance local differences in intensity, since these differences indicate interesting events such as edges.

Example.

Local averaging of weights → Image filtering

more generally:

  • We can take higher order derivatives (2nd & beyond)
  • We can combine local averaging and differences e.g. smooth out noise + take derivatives.
  • We can take arbitrary local linear combinations.
