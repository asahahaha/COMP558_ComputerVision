SIFT Keypoints Issues

Given 2 images,

1. Translation, easy

  • most feature extraction & matching methods are INVARIANT to translation

2. Rotation, hard

  • needs a canonical orientation for each feature
  • compare rotated versions of pics

3. Scaling, harder

  • scale-space theory

SIFT Steps

  1. FIND EXTREMA
    1. LAPLAPLAN
    1. UP-SAMPLE TO GET EXTREMA
  1. FIND HOG FOR EACH EXTREMA
    1. AT THAT SCALE IN PYRAMID
  1. ASSIGN DOMINANT ORIENTATION
  1. COLLECT HOG, GET SIFT

SIFT Steps, details

  • STEP 1: find intensity extrema simultaneously across space(position) & scale (levels in pyramid)
  • STEP 2: downsampling OR upsampling, when comparing across adjacent levels
  • STEP 3: once getting extrema, compute local HoG type feature at the level in the pyramid (THAT scale!!)
    • then assign a dominant orentation
  • STEP 4: collect together HoG type features in a SIFT vector

SIFT STEP 1: position & scale

  • lecture 7: a “box” will produce a local minimum (bright box) or a local maximum (dark box) in a Laplacian of a Gaussian scale space.
  • These local maxima or minima will define the position and scale of SIFT ‘keypoints’.
  • BUT Most maxima and minima detected in a Laplacian of a Gaussian real images are not boxes.
    • analogous to “corner detection”, where the actual goal was to find locally distinctive points.

With SIFT, the goal is to find points 
that produce minima or maxima in scale space (“scale invariant feature transform”).
