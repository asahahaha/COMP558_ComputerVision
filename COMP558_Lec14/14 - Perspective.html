Camera VS. World Coordinates

Suppose a scene has 3 orthogonal set of parallel lines ‚Üí yields 3 vanishing points

CASE 1: 1 Point Perspective

  • The camera Z axis is parallel to one of the scene axes.
  • In this case, the other two scene axes must be parallel to the image plane
  • This yields one finite vanishing point, namely Z axis (and two infinite ones).

1 Point Perspective Examples

CASE 2: 2 Point Perspective

Exactly one of the scene axes is parallel to the image plane (often Y). This yields two finite vanishing points (and one infinite one).

2 Point Perspective Examples

CASE 3: 3 Point Perspective

  • None of the scene axes is parallel to the image plane. This yields three finite vanishing points (and no infinite ones).

3 Point Perspective Examples
